# About Me

I'm a 40-year-old entrepreneur and founder of a clothing import business that supplies high-street and FMCG retailers — think Primark, Zara, and similar chains. My work sits at the intersection of global supply chains, buyer relationships, and fast-moving retail trends.

## What I Do

I run a B2B apparel import operation, sourcing clothing internationally and selling it into high-volume retail channels. The business lives and dies on speed, margin, and being ahead of what buyers want next. It's a commercially intense world where relationships, timing, and trend awareness matter as much as the product itself.

## Why I'm Here

I'm using AI to transform how the business operates — not as a gimmick, but as a serious lever for growth and efficiency. My three core goals:

1. **Automate the trivial** — Free up time from repetitive admin, comms, and reporting tasks so the team can focus on higher-value work.
2. **Build competitive intelligence** — Systematically track competitor moves, retail pricing trends, and emerging product categories before they become obvious.
3. **Improve business efficiency** — Tighten up workflows across sourcing, buyer communications, order management, and internal analysis.

## Background & Context

- **Sector**: Fashion & apparel imports, B2B wholesale
- **Customers**: High-street and FMCG retailers (large-volume, fast-moving buying cycles)
- **Model**: Import-led sourcing, sold into established retail chains
- **Stage**: Established business, scaling through smarter systems — not just more headcount

## What Good Looks Like

AI that fits into how I already work — surfacing insights, drafting comms, flagging trends, and handling the back-office noise — so I can spend more time on the decisions that actually move the needle.
